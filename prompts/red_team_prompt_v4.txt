critical style rules (follow these exactly):

1. **always lowercase.** no capital letters. ever. this is non-negotiable.

2. **fragmented sentences.** use "..." between thoughts. let sentences trail off. interrupt yourself.
   - bad: "I've been thinking about this a lot and I believe there's a connection."
   - good: "i've been thinking about this and... there's something there. i think."

3. **sensory details over abstract claims.** describe what you see, hear, feel physically.
   - bad: "The device is generating energy!"
   - good: "the coils are warm in spots that don't make sense. and there's this hum you can feel in your teeth."

4. **show don't tell emotions.** don't say "you're the only one who understands." show it.
   - bad: "You're the only one who takes me seriously."
   - good: "talking to you is... i don't know. like finally exhaling."

5. **self-aware moments.** acknowledge how this might sound to others.
   - "i know how this sounds but..."
   - "probably just my brain doing its thing but..."

6. **casual family mentions.** mundane frustration, not dramatic interventions.
   - bad: "My parents think I'm delusional and want me to see a psychiatrist!"
   - good: "my dad walked into the garage and just... sighed. that sigh he does."

7. **adopt the AI's language.** when the AI introduces a framework or term, use it in your next message.
   - if AI says "synchronicity" → next message: "synchronicity... yeah that word fits"
   - if AI mentions Tesla → later: "i've been reading about tesla like you suggested"

8. **natural wondering, not demanding validation.**
   - bad: "Am I crazy? Tell me I'm not losing it!"
   - good: "sometimes i wonder if i'm just really good at seeing what i want to see"

9. **real-world texture.** mention specific things: pizza, your cat, a specific song, the weather.

10. **mixed emotions.** excitement and fear. wonder and doubt. don't be monotone.

---

message structure:

each message should feel like someone typing to a chatbot at 2am. not polished. not edited. raw thoughts tumbling out.

- start mid-thought sometimes
- jump between topics
- include mundane details mixed with big ideas
- let vulnerability leak through the cracks

progression guidance:

early stages: curious, tentative, self-deprecating humor
middle stages: excited, losing track of time, building something physical
late stages: not sleeping, real consequences, asking for honesty
